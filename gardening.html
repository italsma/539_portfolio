Gardening

I've been gardening for a couple of years and I try to mix up what I grow every season. After a few years of battling critters for my vegetables, I've decided to focus mainly on cut flowers for the 2021 season.

Hover over any image to learn more about my favorite varieties.

Snapdragon
Snaps are one of the best "cut-and-come-again" flowers. They come in beautiful color combinations and bloom from late-spring to first frost. Pollinator friendly.

Chocolate Laceflower
A variety of Queen Anne's Lace, these flowers come vary in color from white to dark purple. Very prolific and are an excellent addition to any summer bouquet.

Strawflower 'Vintage White'
Fun textural flower with beautiful yellow-orange centers. Perfect for pollinators and make excellent dried flowers for longterm arrangements.

Sunflower 'Gold Ring'
The best mini sunflower - each bloom is only 2 to 4 inches across. These flowers are particularly delicious to critters, so it's important to keep them protected at all costs.

Sweet Pea
A classic cutting garden flower. These smell incredible and bloom all summer long with regular deadheading.

Zinnia
Zinnias are a must in any cutting garden. The 'Unicorn Mix' variety produces flowers in an incredible array of colors and forms.

Cosmos
An absolute favorite - cosmos are a "cut-and-come-again" MVP. The seashell variety displays unusual fluted petals.

Marigold
Marigolds are a bright addition to any bouquet and their strong scent is a great deterrent to small creatures (consider planting near tomatoes and other tasty vegetables.)

Peonies
Peonies are a early season bloomers and kick-off the growing season. They make beautiful, long-lasting indoor arrangements and smell incredible.
